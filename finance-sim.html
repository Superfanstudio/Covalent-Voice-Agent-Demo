03The cost of the AI layer
An AI-native org isn't free — the agents that replace headcount consume tokens, tooling and platform. This charges that cost back to the P&L so the EBITDA you read is net of AI. Defaults are benchmark-anchored; tune them to your own contracts.

AI cost levers

Three drivers, each tunable. AI cost grows as you go AI-native.
Agent tooling per active seat$12K/yr
Copilot + agent stack per remaining human. Market: basic copilots ~$30–50/user/mo; premium agentic seats $300–1,000+/mo.
Agent run-cost per displaced FTE$45K/yr
Tokens + compute + orchestration to run agents doing the work a person used to. A fraction of a $150–750K loaded human; LLMflation drops per-task cost ~10×/yr.
AI platform & infrastructure1.25% of revenue
Orchestration, data platform, security, model routing, evals/observability. Internal-ops layer — far below the ~23% of revenue a pure-AI product spends on inference.

What grounds these numbers

  • ICONIQ State of AI (Jan 2026): model inference ≈ 23% of total AI product cost; AI-native product gross margins ≈ 52% in 2026 vs 75–85% for SaaS.
  • a16z "LLMflation": ~10× annual decline in token cost for equal performance — but total spend still rises as usage expands (Jevons).
  • Dr. M's directive: model AI & token cost from the outset to protect Covalify's 80%+ gross margin — that product-COGS inference sits inside blended GM here, separate from this internal-ops line.
